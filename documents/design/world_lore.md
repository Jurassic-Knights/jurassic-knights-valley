# Jurassic Knights: Valley - World Lore Bible

**The definitive guide to the history, society, geography, and military assets of the Valley.**

---

## 1. The World State

### Core Concept & Tone ("Mud and Iron")
The world is a fusion of **Late WW1 Trench Warfare** and **Medieval Knight Culture**, grounded in gritty reality.
-   **The Conflict**: An endless, stalemated war of attrition in a ruined world.
-   **The Technology**: Bolt-action rifles, gas masks, and heavy artillery exist alongside plate armor, swords, and chivalric codes.
-   **Tone**: Historical war documentary meets epic knightly chronicle. Desaturated, utilitarian, weathered.
-   **The Beasts**: Knights ride dinosaurs (*Triceratops*, *T-Rex*) instead of horses. These are treated as heavy military assets, living tanks, or beasts of burdenâ€”not pets.

### History (Brief)
1.  **The Dawn Age**: Humans and dinosaurs evolved together on this continent. Early civilizations tamed beasts alongside horses.
2.  **The First Orders**: Medieval knight orders formed, with dinosaurs serving as heavy cavalry and living war machines.
3.  **The Current Age**: The "Great War" era. A perpetual deadlock of trench warfare, industrialization, and mechanized chivalry.

### Societal Rules & Reality
**The "No Magic" Reality**:
-   **Physics & Will**: No spells or supernatural elements. Everything is biology, technology (WW1 era), or psychology.
-   **Replacements**:
    -   "Buffs" = Radio commands, rallying cries, adrenaline.
    -   "Magic Effects" = Chemical flares, signal whistles.
    -   "Auras" = Polished brass, spotlights, sheer charisma.

**Populations**:
-   **Humans**: The bulk of infantry/labor. Always shielded by masks/helmets (The Helmet Mandate).
-   **Saurians (Anthro)**: Sapient, speaking reptilian humanoids. Serve as equals (officers/soldiers). No masks needed.
-   **Primals (Feral)**: Non-sapient animals. Used as mounts, tanks, or haulers. Treated as military assets.

## 2. Factions & Society

### The Big Three Factions
1.  **The Iron Vanguard** (The Stalwart Defense)
    -   *Philosophy*: "Hold the Line." Attrition, engineering, firepower.
    -   *Aesthetic*: Industrial grey steel, rivets, trench coats.
    -   *Units*: Trench Fusiliers, Anky Tanks, Siege Engineers.
2.  **The Amber Covenant** (The Ancient Order)
    -   *Philosophy*: "Honor the Pact." Tradition, discipline, bond between Man and Surian.
    -   *Aesthetic*: Polished bronze, worn leather, ceremonial robes.
    -   *Units*: Raptor Cavalry, Saurian Shock-Troopers, Paladins on Trikes.
3.  **The Ashes** (Scavengers & Mercenaries)
    -   *Philosophy*: "Survive the Rot." Ruthless opportunism.
    -   *Aesthetic*: Rusted scrap, mismatched gear, skull motifs.
    -   *Units*: Tunnel Rats, Scavenger Raiders.

### Social Hierarchy
1.  **High Command**: Warlords & Marshals (Authority).
2.  **The Armor**: Tank Knights (Elite Pilots).
3.  **The Infantry**: Fusiliers & Shock Troopers (Frontline).
4.  **Logistics Corps**: Sappers & Mechanics (Support).
5.  **The Survivors**: Refugees & Laborers (Labor).

**Core Virtues**: Awareness, Discipline, Negotiation, Willpower, Tactics, Authority.

## 3. Geography & Locations

### Regions
1.  **The Front (The Grinder)**:
    -   Endless trenches, mud, gas pockets.
    -   **Landmark**: *Point Zero* (Toxic crater lake).
2.  **The Ruined Cities (The Boneyards)**:
    -   Urban warzones, collapsing buildings, ambush predators.
    -   **Landmark**: *Cathedral of Gears* (Ruined factory-church).
3.  **The Hinterlands (Supply Lines)**:
    -   Rear echelons, farms, citadels. Strictly rationed.
    -   **Landmark**: *Ironhold* (Vanguard Fortress).

### Key Location Types
-   **Mud Pots & Field Tents**: Rank-and-file shelter.
-   **Wood Barracks & Stone Bunkers**: Permanent front-line fortifications.
-   **Fortress HQ**: Safe seats of power.

## 4. The War Machine (Economy)

### Scavenging & Gathering
Resources are extracted from disputed sectors:
-   **Fossil Fuel (Coal)**: Fuel.
-   **Trench Timber**: From "Dead Forests".
-   **Rough Stone**: Quarried ruins.
-   **Scrap Iron**: Salvaged battlefield metal.
-   **Primal Meat/Eggs**: Harvested from beasts.

### The Field Forge
Processes materials into:
-   **Trade Goods**: Rations, Munitions (sold for **Gold**).
-   **Equipment**: Weapons, Armor.

### Hatchery Operations
-   **Egg Acquisition**: Found in wild or bred in Pens.
-   **Tiering**: Combine eggs to upgrade potential.
-   **Incubation**: Hatch new Primals for mounts or breeding stock.

## 5. Military Assets

### Bestiary (Tactical Classification)
*Dinosaurs classified by utility, not biology.*

**Class: Assault (Shock Troops)**
-   **Raptor ("Trench Raider")**: Fast scout/attack. Leather barding.
-   **Rex ("Siege Breaker")**: Anti-armor battering ram. Heavy plate jaws.
-   **Carnotaurus ("Pursuit Lancer")**: Flanking cavalry. Speed over durability.

**Class: Armor (Mobile Cover)**
-   **Trike ("Shield-Bearer")**: Mobile infantry cover. Breaching tool.
-   **Anky ("Mobile Bunker")**: Heavy weapons platform (Howitzers/HMGs).
-   **Stegosaurus ("Trench Sweeper")**: Clearer of barbwire/palisades. Auto-cannons.

**Class: Logistics (Support)**
-   **Sauropod ("Heavy Hauler")**: Living cranes/trains.
-   **Hadrosaur ("Pack Mule")**: Ambulance/Transport.

**Class: Air Corps**
-   **Pteranodon ("Sky-Eye")**: Recon/Spotter.
-   **Quetzal ("Heavy Bomber")**: Strategic bombardment.

**Class: Vermin**
-   **Compy ("Trench Rat")**: Pest/Scavenger.
